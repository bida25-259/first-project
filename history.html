History of BMW

BMW, short for Bayerische Motoren Werke, has a rich history of luxury cars, motorcycles, and high-performance engineering. Founded in 1916 in Germany, BMW initially produced aircraft engines during World War I.

1920s – First Cars & Motorcycles

BMW produced its first car, the Dixi, in 1928 and the first motorcycle, the R32, in 1923. These models established BMW’s reputation for engineering excellence.

1930s – Luxury & Motorsport

The BMW 328 became a legendary sports car, competing successfully in races and showcasing BMW’s dedication to performance and design.

1950s–1960s – Post-War Recovery

After World War II, BMW rebounded with “The New Class” sedans, such as the BMW 2002, marking the beginning of modern BMW design and engineering principles.

1970s–1980s – The M Era

BMW launched its M division, introducing iconic performance models like the M3 and M5, cementing BMW’s legacy in motorsports and luxury performance.

1990s–Present – Modern Innovation

BMW continued to innovate with luxury, performance, and technology, introducing electric and hybrid models like the BMW i8, as well as the modern M series, including the BMW M4.

BMW Today

BMW now produces a wide range of vehicles, including SUVs, electric cars, and high-performance sports cars, maintaining its reputation as “The Ultimate Driving Machine.”
